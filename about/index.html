introduction of the organization

Social Entrepreneurs Women’s Alliance (SEWA) Nepal is a nonprofit making, nonpolitical and non-governmental organization which was established in 2022 in District Administration office Kathmandu and also affiliated with Social Welfare Council. It is national level organization of social entrepreneurs. It is dedicated to improving sustainable livelihood of women entrepreneurs, smallholder farmers and backward community based on agriculture and livestock enterprises by strengthening of technical, governance, financial management and organizational of social entrepreneurs. SEWA Nepal is an apex body formed by more than 200 social entrepreneurs' women cooperatives (SEWCs) with 2,00 000 members of six province of Nepal.

Our Objectives

  • To improve rural livelihood and economic prosperity by strengthening the capacity of primary women cooperatives.
  • Advocacy to increase participation of social entrepreneurs in policy making process in all levels of government (Local, province and federal)
  • To increase access to education, health, nutrition, insurance, input supply, market and access to finance through the women empowerment social capital development, social transformation and awareness raising.
  • To facilitate producer for consumer friendly quality production, processing and marketing of Agriculture-livestock products.
  • Develop leadership of women involve in agriculture and livestock business by forming group and networks to develop social entrepreneurship and improve livelihood

Mission and Vision

  • Provide facilitation on institutional development and capacity building of cooperative.
  • Provide facilitation to cooperative about good governance and economic transparency.
  • SEWA will be developed as a national level Resource Center that helps to institutional development of the cooperative.
  • Support to cooperatives for marketing agriculture products with well processing, branding and packaging.
  • Develop organizational strategy plan.
  • Establishment and strengthening of province level network and offices.
  • Establish district branch offices at 77 districts of seven provinces.
  • Affiliation to 1500 cooperatives with SEWA.
  • Establishment of organic fertilizer and grass seed industry.
